The Memories

Track 01: The Server Icon

It started in the digital world of Discord. A simple request for a server icon, but neither of us knew we were actually designing the foundation of our entire universe.

Track 02: The Accidental Lifeline

You were sitting in the dark of that store room, fighting your heaviest thoughts. Then came my clumsy mistake—an accidental camera click. You hadn't seen my face, just a blurry view of me erasing a table. But it made you laugh. That laugh broke the darkness and quietly sparked a love that saved you.

Track 03: The Gravity of Differences

Months later, you poured your heart out to me. I was terrified. Our ages, our religions, our entirely different worlds—it felt impossible. I said no because my heart wasn't ready to risk it, but yours was completely certain.

Track 04: A Night of Prayers

You didn't fight my rejection with anger; you fought it with pure devotion. You stayed awake the entire night, praying to the universe just to keep your reason to smile safe. And the next day, the universe listened.

Track 05: March 28, 2025

The day my walls finally fell. I was too nervous to say it live on our call. That single voice note changed our lives forever: "Call pe toh nhi bol paye per yaha bol dete h... I love you Saiz."

Track 06: Tomorrow & Always

From a screen to holding each other's hearts. Tomorrow, we celebrate the day you were born—the boy who prayed for a smile, and ended up becoming my whole world.

A Note for Midnight

To my dearest Sarib,

When I think about March 9, 2009, I realize the universe was already writing our story long before we ever logged into Discord. It was preparing a soul that would eventually collide with mine in the most unexpected way.

There was a time when the heavy darkness of that store room almost took you away before I even knew who you were. But fate has a beautiful, stubborn way of working. You always tell me that I saved you that day, but the truth is, Saiz, your love has been my sanctuary too.

I was terrified at first. The differences between us felt like walls too high to climb. I tried to protect myself by saying no, but your heart was entirely certain. You stayed awake, pouring your soul into prayers just to keep our connection alive, and those prayers were louder than my fears.

Then came March 28, 2025. My walls crumbled. Call pe toh nhi bol paye per yaha bol dete h... I love you Saiz. That voice note was my surrender, and it was the best decision of my life.

Happy Birthday, Sarib. Here is to 17, to the boundaries we shattered, and to all the midnights we have yet to share.

Forever yours,
Shreya
